Our Story

One school, one motto, every stage of childhood

Bishop Bowers School walks with a child from their very first day in Nursery, through Lower and Upper Primary, and on to Junior High School and the BECE. We are a private Catholic (Christian) school, and that formation — academic, moral and spiritual — runs through everything we do, guided day to day by our religious community and a committed team of teachers and staff.

The school was founded in 1975 by the Missionary Sisters Servants of the Holy Spirit (SSpS), who brought pupils and teachers from St. Theresa's Catholic School in Kaneshie to a new home in Lartebiokorshie. It carries the name of Bishop Joseph Oliver Bowers, SVD, the second Catholic Bishop of the then Accra Diocese. Sister Virginalis, SSpS served as our first Headmistress, and the school continues today under the leadership of Sister Matilda Quist, SSpS, within the Catholic Archdiocese of Accra. In 2025, Bishop Bowers School celebrated fifty years of holistic Catholic education with a golden jubilee.

Bishop Bowers School follows Ghana's national curriculum from Nursery through JHS, with Catholic formation built into every day.

Ages 3 – 5

Nursery

  • Phonics and early literacy built through songs, stories and guided play.
  • Early numeracy — numbers, shapes, sorting and counting.
  • Fine and gross motor skill development through hands-on activity.
  • Social skills: sharing, turn-taking and following simple daily routines.
  • Morning devotion and simple Bible stories woven into the day.

Basic 1–3 · Ages 6 – 8

Lower Primary

  • English Language, Mathematics, Science and Our World Our People.
  • Religious and Moral Education woven into daily lessons and assembly.
  • Daily reading practice and steady handwriting development.
  • Creative arts, music and physical education each week.
  • Class prayer and devotion to open and close the school day.

Basic 4–6 · Ages 9 – 11

Upper Primary

  • Growing depth across English, Mathematics, Science and Social Studies.
  • Introduction to ICT and basic computing skills.
  • Study skills, homework routines and independent learning habits.
  • Clubs and competitions, including events like our Junior Speller spelling bee.
  • Preparation for the step up into Junior High School.

Basic 7–9 · Ages 12 – 14

Junior High School

  • The full JHS curriculum across core and elective subjects.
  • Structured BECE preparation, with mock exams and focused revision.
  • Leadership opportunities — prefects, class representatives and school events.
  • Continued faith formation through catechism, Mass and reflection.
  • Career guidance as pupils prepare for Senior High School.

What to Bring

Documents needed at application
